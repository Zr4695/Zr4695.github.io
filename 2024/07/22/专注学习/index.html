专注学习

发表于2024-07-22|更新于2024-07-22
|阅读量:

为什么你要更专注学习而不是娱乐？

关注备用号，我们不走丢

你学习的质量决定了你成功的质量，然而大多数人花在娱乐和电子产品上的时间与金钱，比花在学习上还多。世界上最成功的人往往都是终身学习者，他们完全明白他们的教育水平直接决定了他们的生活质量。如果终其一生我们都只是娱乐的奴隶，是互联网的工具，永远只会一文不名，难以体验到充实的生活。

Most people are distracted right now. They’re distracted while they’re at work. They’re distracted when they’re with family and friends. They’re distracted at the gym, on their commute, and even in the shower. The mediocre majority will continue going through life this way, never experiencing the fullness of a life filled with deep focus and purpose.

如今大多数人都会经常会分心，他们在工作的时候会分心，当他们与家人和朋友在一起时会分心，他们在健身房会分心，在上下班的路上，甚至在洗澡的时候。平庸的大多数人将继续以这种方式度过一生，从未体验过由高度集中和目标充实的生活。

Most people don’t prioritize learning and creating. They don’t care enough about any efforts to invest in their personal development and growth. Entertainment is more important. Most people have replaced achieving their life dreams and goals with TV, partying, and social media. Their life is characterized by entertainment and distraction, not learning and creating.

大多数人不会优先考虑学习和创造，他们不太关心在个人发展和成长方面投入努力，对他们来说娱乐更重要。大多数人已经用电视、聚会和社交媒体代替了实现自己的人生梦想和目标，他们的生活特点是娱乐和分心，而不是学习和创造。

As a result, they don’t have close relationships. They’re stuck in jobs they hate. Their life is on the fast-track to disappointment, and they don’t know what to do. Benjamin Hardy once said, “Entertainment and distraction is the enemy of creation and learning. They will keep you in mediocrity.” If you don’t want to end up living a life of mediocrity, focus on learning and education. It’s the fastest way to become extraordinary, wealthy, and successful.

因此，他们没有亲密的关系，他们被困在自己讨厌的工作中，他们的生活很快就会陷入失望，他们不知道该做些什么。本杰明·哈迪曾经说过:“娱乐和分心是创造和学习的敌人，它们会让你平庸”。如果你不想过平庸的生活，就要关注学习和教育，这是变得非凡、富有、成功的最快方式，你学习的质量决定了你成功的质量。

Your income, relationships, and success are determined by your learning. Most people spend more money on entertainment and gadgets than self-education. This is why they remain poor and broke with superficial relationships. The quality of your learning and how much you invest in yourself directly determines the extent of your growth. In the words of Ryan Holiday: “The extent of the struggle determines the extent of the growth.” You are defined by what you’re willing to struggle for.

你的收入、人际关系和成功都取决于你的学习，大多数人花在娱乐和电子产品上的钱比花在学习上的钱还多，这就是为什么他们仍然贫穷、一文不名，只有肤浅的人际关系。你学习的质量和你在自己身上投入的多少直接决定了你的成长程度，用瑞恩·霍利迪的话来说: “奋斗的程度决定成长的程度”，你愿意为之奋斗的事物决定了你本身。

Most people aren’t willing to really struggle for anything. They blindly accept what they’re given, and spend their free time disengaging from reality. Renowned motivational speaker Jim Rohn once said the most successful people in the world are always lifelong learners. They fully understand that their level of education directly determines their quality of life.

大多数人不愿意为任何事情去奋斗，他们盲目地接受把空闲的时间花费在脱离现实上，著名的励志演说家吉姆·罗恩曾经说过：世界上最成功的人都是终身学习者，他们完全明白他们的教育水平直接决定了他们的生活质量。

“Formal education makes a living, but self-education makes a fortune,” Rohn explained. It’s not just about a college degree. Most of what you learn in college isn’t applicable to real-world success. It’s your self-education, the kind of learning that helps you develop into the next evolution of yourself. Most people will continue to go through life “in quiet desperation, with their songs left unsung.” Others dictate their income, their happiness, and their fulfillment.

“正规教育可以维持生，但自我教育可以使你富有”罗恩解释道。这不仅仅是一个大学学位，你在大学里学到的大部分东西并不适用于现实世界的成功，是你的自我教育，这种学习才能帮助你进化成为下一个自己。大多数人会继续生活在“无声的绝望中，他们的歌曲不为人知”，另一些人则支配他们的收入、幸福和满足感。

If you don’t want to end up with regret on your death-bed, choose self-education over entertainment. Darren Hardy, author of The Compound Effect, once told a story about how he ended up with his wife. When he was in his 20’s, he began compiling an enormous list of every attribute he wanted his future wife to have. He eventually filled 40 pages detailing the most exquisite and perfect match he could think of. At the end of his writing, he realized something very important: Any woman like that wouldn’t want to have anything to do with someone like him! Hardy realized he needed to become a far better version of himself to attract a woman like that.

如果你不想在临终前还有遗憾的话，选择自学而不是娱乐，我们吸引的东西能反映我们是谁。达伦·哈迪，《复利效应》的作者曾经讲过一个故事，关于他和他的妻子，当他20多岁的时候他开始编制一份庞大的清单，列出了他希望未来妻子拥有的所有品质，他最终写了40页的细节，详细描述了他所能想到的最精致、最完美的搭配，在他写作的最后，他意识到一件非常重要的事情：像那样的女人是不会想和他这样的人有任何瓜葛的! 哈迪意识到他需要成为一个更好的自己来吸引这样的女人。

You must become a far better version of yourself to achieve the enormous success you want. Benjamin Hardy put it this way: “We attract into our lives what we are.” Ask yourself — if you want a million dollars, do you have the mindset of a millionaire? Do you have the financial knowledge and self-discipline to own that kind of money? Most people don’t, yet they continue complaining about being poor.

你必须成为更好的自己才能获得你想要的巨大成功。本杰明·哈迪是这样说的: “什么样的人过什么样的生活” 问问你自己——如果你想要一百万美元，你有百万富翁的思维模式吗? 你有理财知识和自律能力来拥有这笔钱吗? 大多数人都没有，然而他们还是抱怨着自己很穷。

You’ll never become a millionaire if you don’t become someone who could be a millionaire! Every day, millions of people attract mediocre

opportunities into their lives — jobs, time investments, dating partners, etc. — not realizing they are attracting exactly who they are! Extremely successful people are magnets of extraordinary opportunities. They are individuals who gravitate towards success, because they are successful themselves.

如果你不成为一个有能力做百万富翁的人，你就永远成不了百万富翁! 每天，无数的人只能吸引平庸的机会进入他们的生活、工作，时间投资，约会对象等等，却没有意识到他们吸引的正是他们自身。极其成功的人会吸引非凡的机会，他们趋向于成功，是因为他们自己就是成功的人。

If you constantly engage in mediocre, subpar activities with toxic individuals, you’ll only receive similar opportunities. You’ll never experience the type of success of the world’s most extraordinary people, because success is something you attract by the person you become. C.S. Lewis once described humans this way: “We are like eggs at present. And you cannot go on indefinitely being just an ordinary, decent egg. We must be hatched or go bad.” You do not have the luxury to “wait” to improve. Like an egg, you’ll either progress into a new version of yourself, or go rotten.There is no other way.

如果你一直和对你无益的人进行平庸甚至低水平的活动，你也只能得到类似的机会，你永远不会体验到那种世界上最非凡的人的成功，因为你只有成为那样的人才能吸引那样的成功，前进到成长或后退到安全。C.S.刘易斯曾经这样描述人类:“我们现在就像鸡蛋，你不可能永远只做一个普通的、体面的鸡蛋，我们必须被孵化，否则就会变质”，你没有奢侈的“等待”来改善。就像一个鸡蛋，你要么进步，成为一个新的自己，要么腐烂，没有别的路可走。

Most people continue choosing security and safety over the choice to become the fullest version of themselves. Louis Sachar said: “So, what’s it going to be — safety, or freedom? You can’t have both.” Right now, you have the choice — step forward into growth and development, or step backwards into safety and security.

大多数人继续选择安全，而不是选择成为最完整的自己。路易斯·萨奇尔说:“那么，你的选择是什么，安全，还是自由?你不能两者兼得”。现在，你可以选择前进迈向成长和发展，或是倒退，回到安全与保障。

Most people step backward, slowly killing their chances to become extraordinary. Seth Godin once said, “The problem is that our culture has engaged in a Faustian bargain,in which we trade our genius and artistry for apparent stability.” The sad truth is: there’s no such thing as security. Money can be taken away (how many times have you heard of a multi-millionaire going bankrupt?). Our health could fail at any time. Millions of people are abruptly fired or laid off every year.

大多数人会倒退，慢慢地扼杀了他们变得非凡的机会，赛斯·高汀曾经说过，“问题在于我们的文化在进行一场浮士德式的交易，在其中我们用我们的天赋和技巧来换取表面上的稳定”。可悲的事实是：根本没有所谓的安全，钱可以被拿走（你已经听过多少千万富翁破产的消息），我们的健康可能随时会垮掉，每年都有数百万人突然被解雇或下岗。

Security isn’t real. We must choose to design our own life, and let go of our addiction to “apparent stability.” If you’ve traded your genius and creativity for “stability,” it’s time to get those back. Learning and self-education aren’t popular. Most people don’t read books, attend seminars, or take even the most basic steps for self-improvement. They would rather choose distraction and entertainment. Most people will never be successful because they will spend their life this way.

安全并不是真的。我们必须选择设计我们自己的生活，摆脱对“表面稳定”的依赖，如果你用你的天赋和创造力换来了“稳定”，那么是时候把它们拿回来了。大多数人都不会经历真正的成功，学习和自我教育并不受欢迎，大多数人不读书，不参加研讨会，甚至不采取最基本的措施来提高自己，他们宁愿选择娱乐消遣。大多数人永远不会成功，因为他们会用这种方式度过他们的一生。

With just a few small changes to your life, you could enter into the top 5—10% of your field. The competition is extremely low! Amazing, extraordinary opportunities are abundant, because so few people ever take the steps to discover them. You can become one of the few truly successful people in the world by taking your self-education and learning seriously. In a world of mediocrity, you can become extraordinary with only a few simple tweaks.

只要在生活中做一些小的改变，你就可以进入你所在领域的前5-10%，竞争并不激烈，惊人的，非凡的机会是有很多的。因为很少有人采取行动去发现它们，你可以成为世界上少数成功人士的一员，只要你认真对待自我教育和学习。在一个平庸的世界里，你可以通过一些简单的调整变得卓越。

So how do you start? The more you keep evolving into better versions

of yourself, the more you’ll need to be focused on the things that are most important. Your days must be characterized by high-quality activities and behaviors. Benjamin Hardy put it this way: “Success is continuously improving who you are, how you live, how you serve, and how you relate.” James Altucher once said, “Every day, check these 4 boxes: Have I improved 1% on physical, emotional, mental, and spiritual health?”

那么如何开始呢? 你越是不断进化成为更好的自己，你就越需要专注于最重要的事情，你的生活中更多的必须是高质量的活动和行为。本杰明·哈迪是这样说的：“成功就是不断完善你是谁，你如何生活，你如何服务，你如何与人相处”。詹姆斯·阿尔图切尔曾经说过“每天，检查这4个盒子：我的身体、情感、心理和精神健康有提高了1%吗？”

You can start small. Even the smallest improvement is more than most people will ever see. While most people change slowly and unconsciously over time, you can begin taking control of your success by choosing to make a few small improvements. That’s all it takes. Extraordinary people are very uncommon. Their lifestyle of learning and self-education aren’t popular, either.

你可以从小事开始，即使是最小的进步，也已经超过了大多数人。当大多数人慢慢地无意识地随时间改变，你可以通过选择做一些小的改进来开始掌控你的成功，这就是全部。如果你想过非凡的生活，你就需要放弃平凡的生活，非凡的人是非常稀少的的，他们的学习和自我教育的生活方式，也同样不流行。

Odds are, once you begin investing in yourself, you’ll find it’s a lonely road. Todd Brison wisely said, “The more bold you are, the more rejection you’ll experience.” The mob of the majority doesn’t like deserters. If you want an extraordinary life, you’ll need to give up many parts of the “normal” life. That might mean giving up common toxic behaviors

or hanging out with toxic people. If “everyone does it,” you’d do well to give it a long, hard look. Most people will never achieve an extraordinary life.

很可能，一旦你开始投资自己，你会发现这是一条孤独的道路。托德·布里森明智地说:“你越勇敢，你遭遇的拒绝也就越多”。多数人的群体并不喜欢离群者，如果你想要非凡的人生，你需要放弃“普通”生活的许多部分，这可能意味着要放弃常见的有害行为，或与不好的人混在一起。如果有的事“每个人都这样做”，那你最好再仔细地审视一下，大多数人永远不会成就非凡的人生。

That’s OK. The extraordinary life isn’t for everyone. Learning and self-education take time, energy, and focus that most people would rather spend on entertainment and distraction — “ordinary” things. “Great” opportunities cost “good” ones; you can’t have both. Benjamin Hardy said: Before you evolve, you can reasonably spend time with just about anyone. You can reasonably eat anything placed in front of you. You can reasonably justify activities and behaviors that are, frankly, mediocre.

这没什么。非凡的人生并不适合每个人，学习和自我教育需要时间、精力和注意力，而大多数人宁愿把这些花在娱乐和消遣上这些“普通”的事情上，“好”的机会需要付出“巨大的”代价，你不能两者兼得。本杰明·哈迪说：在你逐步发展之前，你可以花时间和任何人在一起，你可以吃下任何摆在你面前的东西，你可以合理地为那些平庸的活动和行为辩护。

But as your vision expands, you realize you need to make adjustments. You can’t spend your money on junk anymore. You need to manage your time much more diligently. You need to invest in yourself and your future. To avoid mediocrity, you can’t continue being with negative people. You can’t keep eating crappy foods that slow you down. You can’t do many of the things you used to do. This is what extraordinary requires. Most people see the price and simply say “no thanks.” But you can do anything if you pay the price.

但随着你的视野扩大，你意识到你需要做出调整，你不能再把钱花在垃圾上了，你需要更加努力地管理你的时间，你需要为自己和你的未来投资，为了避免平庸，你不能继续和消极的人在一起，你不能一直吃会给你拖后腿的垃圾食物，你不能做很多你以前做的事情，这就是成为一个非凡的人的要求。大多数人看到需要的代价就会说“不，谢谢”，但如果你付出代价，你可以做任何事情。

There are probably dozens of things you do on a daily basis that aren’t helping you improve. Most people live their lives on other people’s terms. Their days are spent working on other people’s goals and following the rules of someone else.Entertainment and distraction rule society. There will never be a shortage of TV shows, Buzzfeed articles, or Bad Lip Reading videos to take up your time. If you want to avoid mediocrity and achieve enormous success, you must cut out distractions and prioritize learning instead. Most people will go their whole lives as a slave to entertainment.

总结

也许你每天做的很多事情都不能帮助你提高，大多数人都是按照别人的方式生活的，他们把时间花在别人的目标上，遵循别人的规则。娱乐和分心主宰着的社会，永远不会缺少电视节目，Buzzfeed的文章，或BLR的视频来占用你的时间。如果你想避免平庸并取得巨大的成功，你必须排除干扰，把学习放在优先位置，大多数人终其一生，都只是娱乐的奴隶。

They prioritize entertainment over improving their life, their family’s well-being, and their hopes and dreams. This is how people can stay at jobs they hate for years. It’s how people stay in toxic relationships and remain perpetually broke. They don’t bother learning how to succeed. If your lifestyle does not add to your growth and improvement, it will bring you down. Prioritize learning. Invest in self-education. Choose success over mediocrity.

他们优先考虑娱乐，而不是改善他们的生活、他们的家庭幸福、他们的希望和梦想。这就是为什么人们可以在他们讨厌的工作上呆上几年，这就是人们为什么保持有害的关系并永远一文不名，他们懒得去学习如何成功。如果你的生活方式不能促进你的成长和进步，它就会使你堕落。优先学习，投资自我教育，选择成功而不是平庸。
